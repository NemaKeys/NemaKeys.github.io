Reference Orders of Nematodes .

  • Key to the Orders and Suborders of Nematoda:
    • Class Enoplea: Esophagus cylindrical or bottle-shaped; amphids usually post-labial; excretory system single-cell hypodermal glands; male usually with 2 testes; caudal alae rare; phasmids absent
      • Anterior sensillae often in 3 whorls of inner labial papillae, outer labial setae and cephalic setae; metanemes (filamentous structures in lateral chors) present; caudal glands present; esophageal glands open in or near stoma
        • Spicule protractor muscles attached to proximal enlargement; metanemes present; marine and fresh water
          • Enoplida, Filipjev, 1929
          Spicule protractor muscles form a pouch around spicules; protrusible tooth in some forms
          • Triplonchida, Cobb, 1919
      • Anterior sensillae rarely setiform; stoma with teeth or odontostyle; esophagus cylindrical, bottle-shaped or a stichosome; caudal glands usually absent; esophageal glands open in posterior region of esophagus
        • Movable hollow spear; esophagus expanded posteriorly - bottle-shaped.
          • Dorylaimida, Pearse, 1942
        • Glandular esophageal stichosome and intestinal trophosome; juveniles are parasites of invertebrates.
          • Mermithida, Hyman, 1951
        • Esophagus vestigial; parasites of vertebrates in all life stages.
          • Muspiceida, Bain & Chabaud, 1959
        • Esophagus cylindrical throughout.
          • Juveniles are parasites of invertebrates; adults free-living
            • Marimermithida
          • All stages parasitic; adults are vertebrate parasites, juveniles with or without intermediate hosts
            • Posterior esophagus a stichosome; onchiostyle present; monovarial; anphids pore-like
              • Trichinellida Hall, 1916
            • Anterior end has a sucker; males with bell-shaped bursa
              • Dioctophymatida
          • All stages free-living
            • Strong, large, cuticularized stoma with prominent dorsal tooth; predators of nematodes
              • Mononchida Mononchida
            • Long and slender; tubular stoma without teeth; stoma without lips; soil inhabitants
              • Isolaimida Cobb, 1920
    • Class Chromadorea: Esophagus often subdivided into bulbs; amphid apertures on lips, often difficult to see, or posterior to lips and obvious - round or coiled; gonads single or paired in both male and female
      • Amphids usually pore-like, on lips; cephalic sensillae on lip region; phasmids present; caudal glands absent; mainly terrestrial, rarely freshwater or marine; include free-living, animal parasites, plant parsites
        • Rhabditida, Chitwood, 1933
      • Amphids post-labial, varied shapes; phasmids absent; somatic seta common; mainly free-living aquatic organisms
        • Anterior sensillae in three whorls, inner labial, outer labial and cephalic; amphids var from circular to complex spirals, post-labial and usually posterior to cephalic setae; stoma funnel-shaped with a dorsal tooth and two smaller subventral teeth; esophagus cylindrical with posetrior bulb; females diovarial; mainly marine
          • Chromadorida, Chitwood, 1933
